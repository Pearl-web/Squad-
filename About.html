Celebrating Britain's West Indian community since 1966...


The Carnival's roots stem from 1959 when a London-based Trinidadian human rights activist, Claudia Jones, set up an indoor 'Caribbean Carnival' at St Pancras Town Hall.

This led to indoor Caribbean events being held in many halls across London during the 1960s, hosted by Trinidadian couple booking agents, Edric and Pearl Connor, along with partners such as the West Indian Gazette.

The first outdoor carnival took place in 1966, initially for children, with the local West Indian residents involved. The Russ Henderson Steel Band, a popular band in the Caribbean community, were invited and attracted large crowds to the first… Notting Hill Carnival.

Now in it's fifth decade, the Notting Hill Carnival attracts almost two million travellers from around the globe each year over the August bank holiday, celebrating West London's West Indies community through vibrant performances, popular food and eclectic music.
